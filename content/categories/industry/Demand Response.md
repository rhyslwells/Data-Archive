# Demand Response (DR)

**Demand Response (DR)** is the controlled adjustment of electricity consumption in response to:

- Price signals, or
- Dispatch instructions from a system operator or aggregator

Its purpose is to support system balancing, reduce costs, and improve reliability.

If baseline demand is $D(t)$:

$$
D_{actual}(t) = D(t) + \Delta D(t)
$$

where $\Delta D(t)$ is the change in demand induced by DR.

## Role

Electricity systems must satisfy:

$$
Generation(t) = Demand(t) + Losses(t)
$$

DR shifts part of the balancing responsibility from generation to demand.

## Key Idea

DR makes electricity demand ==**flexible and controllable**==, allowing it to be treated as a system resource.
