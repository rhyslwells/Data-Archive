### Data Infrastructure and Governance in Flexible Energy

Flexible energy systems rely on high-frequency telemetry from sensors and control systems. Data quality challenges — including missing values, ==sensor faults==, and anomalous spikes — require automated validation and ==anomaly detection== pipelines. Poor data governance directly degrades forecasting and optimisation reliability.

The volume and velocity of time-series data necessitate distributed processing architectures. Cloud-native infrastructure and parallelised frameworks enable scalable ingestion, streaming analytics, and fault tolerance. Because decisions affect physical infrastructure, resilience is critical.

Interoperability further enhances system coordination. Standardised data models, API-driven architectures, and open data principles reduce ==integration friction== between utilities, aggregators, regulators, and asset owners. Effective data governance underpins scalable [[Flexibility Markets]].
